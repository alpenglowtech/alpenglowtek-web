WHY FPGA FOR PQC

Why FPGA for PQC Acceleration

PQC algorithms (e.g., CRYSTALS-Kyber, CRYSTALS-Dilithium) are highly compute-intensive and memory-access intensive. Executing them on traditional CPUs or GPUs often results in high latency, large power consumption, and inefficiency. Since CPU and GPU hardware architectures are fixed and highly generalized, they are difficult to optimize for specific cryptographic operations.

In contrast, FPGA features reconfigurability, allowing custom hardware architectures based on PQC algorithm requirements. Through highly parallelized computing and dedicated data path design, it achieves lower latency and lower power consumption with fewer resources, balancing flexibility and upgradeability.

CORE ADVANTAGES

Core Technical Advantages

Customized Hardware Architecture Design

Dedicated hardware optimization for polynomial arithmetic, modular arithmetic, and NTT (Number Theoretic Transform) in PQC algorithms, significantly improving computing efficiency.

Low Latency & High Throughput

Through parallelized data paths and pipeline design, significantly reducing encryption/decryption latency and increasing overall system throughput, suitable for real-time secure communication and high-performance applications.

Low Power & Resource Efficiency

FPGA can complete PQC operations with lower hardware resources and power consumption, which is especially suitable for edge devices and embedded systems.

Reconfigurable & Long-Term Security

Supporting algorithm updates and standard evolution, as PQC standards continue to develop, quick upgrades via FPGA reconfiguration reduce system risks and maintenance costs.
